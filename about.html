  • About Us

    Welcome to Matangira Gallery, where stone transforms into timeless masterpieces. We specialize in creating exquisite stone sculptures that embody the essence of true artistry. Each piece is meticulously crafted, reflecting the beauty, culture, and passion behind every chisel and carve.

    Our mission is to bring the soul of stone to life, blending tradition with innovation to create works that inspire and captivate. Whether it's a modern abstract or a classic figure, every sculpture at Matangira Gallery is a seal of true art.

  • History

    Godfrey was born in Wedza on the 5th of March 1975, and was educated at Mukamba Primary School in Buhera District. He is married and has two kids. There he was raised by his grandmother after his mother had passed away. His grandmother was the mother of the late great stone sculpting legend Nicholas Mukomberanwa. During the holidays he used to visit his uncles Nicholas Mukomberanwa, Nesbert Mukomberanwa and Albert Nathan Mamvura- during these visits he would play with sculpting tools as toys, this is when his passion for stone carving was birthed and subsequently nurtured.

    When he was in the 6th grade he was transferred to Chitungwiza, where he stayed with his father. He realised that he was talented in art the same year, after excursions with making wire crafts and wood carvings. As a young boy, he frequented his Uncle Albert Nathan Mamvura's workshop in Ruwa where he met a lot of the first generation Shona sculpture artists such as Sylvester Mubayi and many others who had a huge influence in the shaping of his career as an artist. In 2002, he moved to Nesbert's place, where he worked under his tutelage from 2002 to 2006.

    Godfrey started working on his own in 2007 with the help of the Mukomberanwa family, especially Nicholas Mukomberanwa who encouraged him to work independently. He loves creating unique pieces in different types of medium, particularly the hard type of stones such as springstone, opal, fruit serpentine, butter jade, lepidolite, cobalt and dolomite. He has participated in many exhibitions, competitions and workshops, of special mention is the Brock Awards first prize best sculpture he won in 2017 at Chitungwiza Arts Centre..
